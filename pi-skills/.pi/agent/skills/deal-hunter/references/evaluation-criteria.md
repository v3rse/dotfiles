## Scoring Rubric

Score each deal 1–10 across these factors. Weighted average gives overall score.

| Factor | Weight | 10/10 | 5/10 | 1/10 |
|--------|--------|-------|------|------|
| **Price vs new** | 30% | 50%+ discount | 20% discount | Full price or higher |
| **Condition accuracy** | 20% | "Wie neu" with photos | "Gut" with photos | "Gut" with no photos or vague |
| **Seller trust** | 20% | Commercial refurbisher, 99%+ | Private with history, 95%+ | Unknown, no history, suspicious |
| **Warranty** | 15% | 24+ months | 6–12 months | None |
| **Shipping cost** | 10% | Free | €5–10 | €20+ or unclear |
| **Delivery speed** | 5% | 1–2 days | 3–7 days | 2+ weeks or unknown |

**Overall score interpretation:**
- 9–10: Exceptional deal — buy immediately if budget allows
- 7–8: Good deal — strong contender
- 5–6: Fair deal — acceptable if nothing better found
- 3–4: Weak deal — only if desperate
- 1–2: Avoid — likely scam or terrible value

## Red Flags (Auto-Reject)

Never recommend a listing with any of these:

- **Price <50% of market value** with no clear reason (scam indicator)
- **No photos of actual item** — stock photos only
- **Seller has <90% positive feedback** on eBay or no transaction history
- **Description is copy-paste generic** or in broken German/English
- **Payment methods: only bank transfer or crypto** — no PayPal, no buyer protection
- **"Defekt", "Bastler", "Ersatzteile"** unless user explicitly wants broken items
- **Shipping cost not stated** or "ask seller"
- **Seller refuses to answer questions** or is evasive about condition
- **Computrace/AMT active** on used business laptops (check BIOS)
- **BIOS supervisor password set** on ThinkPads (cannot be reset easily)

## Condition Grades Explained

| Grade | German | Meaning | Typical Discount |
|-------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Like New | Wie neu / Neuwertig | Open box, unused, all accessories | 10–20% |
| Very Good | Sehr gut | Light use, minimal wear, fully functional | 20–35% |
| Good | Gut | Visible use, scratches possible, functional | 35–50% |
| Acceptable | Akzeptabel / Fair | Heavy wear, may have cosmetic issues, functional | 50–65% |
| Defective | Defekt | Broken or partially functional | 70–90% |

**Note:** Grades vary by seller. One shop's "Gut" may be another's "Sehr gut". Photos matter more than the label.

## Seller Trust Levels

| Level | Type | Examples | Risk |
|-------|------|----------|------|
| **A** | Commercial refurbisher with warranty | AfB, refurbed.de, Back Market, Lap-Works | Low — business with reputation |
| **B** | Established eBay seller (1000+ sales, 99%+) | Power sellers, shops | Low-medium — professional |
| **C** | Small eBay seller / KleinAnzeigen commercial | 100+ sales, 95%+ | Medium — some accountability |
| **D** | Private seller with history | eBay/KleinAnzeigen with past sales | Medium-high — no warranty |
| **E** | Unknown / new account | 0–10 sales, no history | High — scam risk |

## New vs Used vs Refurbished Decision Tree

```
Budget tight (<60% of new price)?
  → Look at used + refurbished
Need warranty / return policy?
  → Refurbished (12–36mo) or Amazon Warehouse
Want latest model?
  → New only (used won't exist yet)
Buying for hobby / tinkering?
  → Used is fine, even "Gut" condition
Buying for daily driver / work?
  → Refurbished "Wie neu" or "Sehr gut" minimum
Risk-averse / first purchase?
  → Amazon Warehouse or commercial refurbisher
```

## Price Sanity Checks

Before calling a deal "good", verify:

1. **Check Idealo/Geizhals** for current new price — your "deal" might be full price
2. **Check price history** (camelcamelcamel.de for Amazon, idealo price graph) — is this actually a low price or just normal?
3. **Factor in total cost** — price + shipping + any customs/VAT
4. **Factor in accessories** — does it include charger, box, cables? Missing charger = €15–30 extra
5. **Factor in immediate needs** — will you need to buy RAM, SSD, battery? Add those costs

## When to Stop Searching

Stop and recommend when you find:
- **One 9–10 score deal** — good enough
- **Two 7–8 score deals** with different trade-offs (e.g., cheap vs safe)
- **Nothing above 5** after searching 5+ marketplaces — report that the market is dry

Don't keep searching endlessly. 3–5 solid options is enough for the user to decide.
